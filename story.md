# MICK THE PLUMBER: The Big Clog
### Story bible and level plan, v1 (Ninja Turtles x Grease)

**Logline:** A city plumber gets shrunk to rat-size on the night shift, falls in with a doo-wop rat gang from the undercity, and whips, wrenches, and dances his way to the monster blocking the Great Main: the BIG CLOG.

**Global motifs (every level):**
- The sewer lights blink together **every 4 beats** of the level's song. It reads as atmosphere in early levels; by Level 4 you learn it's the rats' dance cue, and by the finale it's your battle metronome.
- **GLUG.** stays as the drown/game-over screen in every level. It is canon now.
- Snake-whip scenes open with giant letters slamming on screen: **"I GOT A SNAKE, MAN."**
- Valves remain the traversal language everywhere; the whip adds verticality from Level 5 on.

**The Slick Tails:** a rat gang out of the undercity's Eastside Drain. 1950s New York greasers: pompadour fur, combs in jacket pockets, letterman jackets stitched **PS 13** (Public Sewer 13). They never just walk in. Every entrance is choreographed: finger snaps on the 4-beat blink, shoulder rolls, group slide, freeze. Leader: **Ronnie Sleeks** (smooth talker, toothpick). Muscle: **Knuckles** (bends copper pipe for fun). Choreographer: **Twitch** (never stops moving). They help Mick because the Big Clog backed up their whole neighborhood, and because rat grandmas remember the plumber who fixed the Eastside leak for free.

## LEVEL 1 — GRAVEYARD SHIFT
**Song: "Mick the Plumber"** | Scene: the city sewer works (the current playable level: Stone Works, Steel Mains, The Cistern)
**Objective:** Reach and close the Master Valve.
Normal-size Mick on a routine night call: pressure's spiking all over the district. At the Master Valve he cranks it shut, but the line surges and blasts him with glowing green Wonder-Gunk (the same slime that's been dripping all level: foreshadowing). The screen warps. Boots too big. Wrench like a telephone pole. **Mick is three inches tall.** A rat shadow watches from a grate, snaps its fingers twice, and vanishes.

## LEVEL 2 — SMALL TIME
**Song: "sideshow"** | Scene: the same sewer, now gigantic. A mop bucket is an ocean. A matchstick is a girder.
**Objective:** Get off the maintenance shelf and down to the undercity, alive.
Platforming re-taught at tiny scale: soap bubbles are platforms, a drain chain is a climbing rope, a dripping faucet is a timed hazard, a curious cockroach is a rideable bull. The level ends at a storm drain lip where the water below glows with distant string lights: there is a CITY down there. Mick jumps.

## LEVEL 3 — DRIVE TO HOOD
**Song: "drive to hood"** | Scene: drainpipe rapids into Ratsville, an undercity built from the city's losses: graffiti'd brick, fire escapes of pipe fittings, streetlights made of dropped birthday candles.
**Objective:** Ride the paper boat through the rapids and make it to Ratsville's gates.
A rush level: steer a folded newspaper boat through white water, duck low pipes, whip past valve gates Mick opens on the fly. On the banks, greaser rat silhouettes pace the boat, snapping on the 4-beat blink, sizing him up. At the gates, the boat wrecks. Mick washes up at the feet of six letterman jackets.

## LEVEL 4 — THE SLICK TAILS
**Song: "hard beat"** | Scene: the Hubcap Diner, the gang's den: a chrome hubcap for a roof, a harmonica for a jukebox, milk-cap stools, neon from a cracked EXIT sign.
**Objective:** Win the dance-off. Earn the jacket.
No platforming: a rhythm battle. Ronnie Sleeks doesn't trust surface folk, so Twitch calls the steps. Match the gang's moves on the 4-beat blink (the whole level is the beat-sync feature as gameplay). Win, and the rats circle up, stomp twice, and toss Mick a letterman jacket in his size: PS 13, honorary. Ronnie: "The Clog took our block, plumber man. You in?"

## LEVEL 5 — I GOT A SNAKE, MAN
**Song: "friendo"** | Scene: the Boiler Yards, a rusted vertical wasteland between Ratsville and the Great Main.
**Objective:** Cross the Boiler Yards with the gang as escort.
Opens in the gang's trophy room: on the wall, coiled in chrome glory, the legendary **Pipe Snake** (a drain snake, whip-sized at Mick's scale). Giant words slam the screen: **I GOT A SNAKE, MAN.** New verbs: whip-swing from flange hooks, whip-stun pipe crabs, whip-grab distant valve wheels. The Slick Tails run the rooftops alongside, hitting formation poses on every 4-beat blink, kicking barrels off ledges to clear Mick's path. Buddy-movie level. Ends with the crew at a cliff edge, looking across at a wall of hair and grease on the horizon.

## LEVEL 6 — TEMPLE OF THE FROG
**Song: "temple of the frog"** | Scene: the flooded pre-war storm temple under the old district: mossy stone heads, glyph lights blinking the 4-beat, tongue-pink bridges.
**Objective:** Pass the Frog's three trials and learn the Clog's weakness.
The ancient Frog (huge, still, sensei-calm; been down here since before the pipes had names) guards the only route to the Great Main. Three trials: **Patience** (stand still through a drip cycle while crumble grates tempt you to move early), **Rhythm** (cross tongue bridges that only hold on the 4-beat blink), **Flow** (a valve puzzle where water must be routed the long way, not forced). The Frog speaks once: "Grease binds it. Cut the lines, and the water does the rest." He marks Mick's wrench with a glowing lily pad sigil.

## LEVEL 7 — THE BIG CLOG
**Song: "longhair"** | Scene: the Great Main, dammed floor to ceiling by the BIG CLOG: a groaning mountain of grease, lost toys, and fifty years of hair. It has eyes. It is not sorry.
**Objective:** Cut the three grease lines, then open the Master Sluice on it.
Three-phase boss. Phase 1: whip-cut the grease lines while the Clog lobs hairballs (Slick Tails run distraction formations below, and the lights blink the openings every 4 beats). Phase 2: the Clog floods the chamber in waves; valve work on the fly, GLUG lurking on every mistimed dive. Phase 3: ride a geyser to the Master Sluice wheel, whip-grab it, and crank as the whole screen rumbles. The wall of water takes the Clog apart strand by strand. The Great Main runs clear.

## EPILOGUE — SLO MO
**Song: "slo mo plumber reprise"** | Credits scene.
The Wonder-Gunk washes off in the clean water: Mick grows back mid-frame, letterman jacket now a wristband. Sunday night at the Hubcap Diner: slice night. The whole gang, one tiny frog on a stool, and a plumber with a legend. Everyone snaps on the 4-beat. Fade on the GLUG screen used one last time as a joke: GLUG. (of a soda, this time).

## Build notes (for the pipeline, later)
- Level 1 already exists (v2 build). Levels 2-7 are new tilemaps on the same engine; the dance-off (L4) and boat rapids (L3) are new modes worth their own estimate lines.
- Songs referenced: Mick the Plumber, sideshow, drive to hood, hard beat, friendo, temple of the frog, longhair, slo mo plumber reprise. All originals, all in the songs folder.
- The 4-beat light blink should be one shared system (audio.currentTime driven) reused across every level and the dance-off judge.
- Rat sprites: 32x44, pompadour silhouette, jacket two-tone; group choreography is one animation timeline instanced with offsets (cheap, reads as a dance troupe).
- Suggested build order: L4 dance-off (validates beat system as gameplay), then L2, L3, L5, L6, L7.
